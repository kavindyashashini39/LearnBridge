#### Sustainable Development Goal (SDG)

- SDG 4 – Quality Education
- SDG 8 – Decent Work and Economic Growth

#### Reason for Selection

Quality education (access to inclusive, equitable and quality

education is essential for personal and professional development).

The situation is that a large portion of students / job seekers does

not have an affordable and organized online learning platform to

learn about things. **LearnBridge** is specifically tackling this need

by representing educational content in a mobile-based form

problem shown and engaging journey through personalized

learning paths/checks in that form.

Supporting goal, the supporting SDG to be selected for SDG 4

was SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth) since it is

recognized that education is a key factor in increasing

employability and economic productivity. While helping on user

to build industry-relevant skills, certifications and continuous

learning opportunity to make them competitive in the job market.

This adds to decent job prospects, upskilling of the workforce and

sustainable economic growth.


**LearnBridge** can contribute to linking education with work by

equipping individuals with knowledge and capabilities, helping

promote the informed provision of quality education while

indirectly advancing decent work and sustainable economic

growth.

```
“Quality education is the foundation for decent work and sustainable
economic growth; LearnBridge integrates both goals through a single
digital learning platform.”
```

#### Problem statement

- Quite a lot of students particularly in developing countries
  like Sri Lanka are struggling to get access high quality
  education resources, learning pathways and affordable
  digital learning platforms. Many conventional education
  systems fall short of flexibility, personalization and digital
  access. Students are therefore not able to learn at their own
  pace or to acquire 21 st-century job market demand skills.

#### Proposed solution

- The application proposed- LearnBridge is intended to solve
  these problems by bringing the two - Learners and quality
  education, closer. LearnBridge is a one-stop shop digital
  learning platform that allows users to search for courses,
  sign up for learning initiatives, check their progress and
  perform educational tasks via a user-friendly mobile solution.
- All UI designs are done in Figma and emphasize on
  simplicity, clarity, usability and consistency throughout the
  screens.

#### Target Users

- School students
- University and college students
- Self-learners and job seekers
- Educators and educational content providers


**LearnBridge** Freemium and Subscription-based business model
The LearnBridge mobile application operates with a modified
Freemium combined with a subscription-based revenue model to
make the app easily accessible to users, while being commercially
viable in terms of the long-run perspective.

This model provides basic free functions, such as searching
courses, previewing introductory topics, and monitoring learning
progress. The app is free to use, which means there are no
barriers to entry for students and enthusiasts who'd like to
familiarize themselves with the software.

Premium features are provided in subscription plans on a monthly
or yearly basis. These premium qualities are access to higher level
and professional courses, downloadable learning files, certificates
of completion that are recognized, tailored learning methods in
addition to analytics for progress. For user that want to
strengthen their skills and job opportunities, there is also a
premium plan that users could upgrade.

This business model fits LearnBridge perfectly, given that
students, job seekers and lifelong learners are not used to
investing in their learning without testing it’s quality first. This
freemium model provides an opportunity to become familiar with
the app, while the subscription concept provides a method for
supporting content development, platform maintenance and
ongoing improvement.

#### Screens Covering All CRUD Functionalities

The Figma design for the LearnBridge mobile app contains a full

suite of screens to cover the basic CRUD functionalities needed

for Learning Content Management. Each screen is named and laid

out to clearly support how users might interact with it effectively

and intuitively:

1. **Create Screen – “Add New Course”**
   o Allows users to input course details such as title,
   description, category, and optionally upload a
   thumbnail or image.
   o Includes form elements, text fields, drop-downs, and a
   clear “Save” button to create new course entries.
2. **Read Screen – “Course List”**
   o Displays all available courses in a list or card view
   format.
   o Each item shows a course title, short description,
   category, and progress indicator.
   o Users can tap any course to view details.
3. **Update Screen – “Edit Course”**
   o Provides edit functionality for existing course details.
   o Pre-populates the current course information and
   allows users to modify fields.
   o A clear “Update” button is presented to save changes.


4. **Delete Option – “Delete Course” Confirmation**
   o Provides a formatted confirmation prompt when a user
   attempts to delete a course.
   o The dialogue includes “Cancel” and “Confirm Delete”
   responses to prevent accidental deletion.

#### Proper Labeling of Screens and Features

The design consistently uses clear and descriptive labels for each
screen and in-app feature to enhance usability and evaluation
clarity

- Screen titles such as **“Add New Course,” “Course List,”**
  **“Edit Course,”** and **“Delete Confirmation”** clearly identify
  each function.
- Action buttons are labeled with directive text such as

```
Save
```
```
Update
```

**Cancel**

- Form fields and UI elements include descriptive labels
  like “Course Title,” “Category,” “Description,” and
  “Upload Image” to remove ambiguity for the user.
- Navigation elements (e.g., back arrows, menu icons) are
  visually consistent across screens and labeled where
  necessary for clarity.


This attention to labeling improves both user experience and
instructor evaluation, as it clearly reflects the intention and
capability of each screen.

- The app uses a **uniform visual language** across
  screens, including consistent color schemes,
  typography, and spacing.
- Primary and secondary actions are distinguished by
  consistent styling (e.g., filled buttons for primary actions
  like Save/Update, outlined for secondary actions like
  Cancel).
- The flow between screens — from listing to creating to
  editing — is intuitive and supports the natural CRUD
  process.


The LearnBridge mobile app project is a group-based project
including four (4) person. To balance effort and prevent
overlapping of work assignment, the twenty-four (24) total user
interface screens were evenly divided among the team members.

The overall project was divided such that every group member
assumed responsibility of designing the functionality of a distinct
module with all CRUD (Create, Read, Update and Delete)
operations interfaces as well as other auxiliary UI screens. This
organized allocation contributes to a fair workload division and a
full application coverage.
